    Characterizing the degradation of apatite waste form material (Experimental)

    This project is to characterize the mechanism of iodine release from iodoapatite Pb9.85(VO4)6I1.7, a promising waste form to immobilize radionuclide iodine-129, in aqueous environments. I conducted semi-dynamic leaching tests (ASTM C1308) on bulk samples in cap-sealed Teflon vessels filled with aqueous solutions, imitating various environmental conditions. The collected aliquots were analyzed by ICP-MS and ICP-OES, and the chemical and physical alterations to samples were characterized by XRD, SEM-EDS, TEM microscopy, Raman, and infrared spectroscopy. Solution and surface characterization showed that the iodine release is controlled by both short-term diffusion and long-term congruent dissolution processes, and the release rate is highly sensitive to the solution chemistry such as solution pH, ionic species, and saturation state. In addition, I observed formation of secondary phases, such as Pb2V2O7 from pH 4, Pb5(VO4)3Cl from NaCl, and Pb5(VO4)3OH from pH 10. The findings of this project provide a theoretical framework for improving long-term disposal safety of nuclear waste. I made an award-wining educational video on this topic.
